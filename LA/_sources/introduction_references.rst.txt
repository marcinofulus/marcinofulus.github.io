As an important branch of mathematics, Linear Algebra has 
an immense bibliography, both in traditional (paper) and 
electronic (Internet) media. On the other hand, the information
on textbooks, and very often the textbooks themselves, are easily accessed.
A comprehensive list of introductory and advanced textbooks is given 
in the article on Linear Algebra in Wikipedia_. 

.. For this reason we do not intend to give a vast list of references 
   on the subject. Instead, we only mention a few items which seem 
   interesting to us.

Thus, instead of reproducing a full list of references,
we shall only mention a few items which seem to us 
especially interesting and recommendable.

The most noteworthy are the books by Gilbert Strang. [1]_ [2]_
and his lectures MIT 18.06 Linear Algebra, Spring 2005, accessible
on YouTube_ or directly in the MIT_ website.

Another compelling item is a friendly written and interactive, Sage-based 
textbook on Linear Algebra by Robert A. Beezer, available online_.
