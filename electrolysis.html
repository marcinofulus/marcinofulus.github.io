Why do reactions stall at low voltage?

  • Decomposition Voltage: Electrolysis is forced; reactions require a minimum theoretical voltage to start.
  • Overpotential: Real-world resistance requires extra voltage beyond the theoretical minimum to actually form gases or metals.
  • Capacitor Effect: Below the threshold, ions briefly align but can't trade electrons. No steady current flows.
