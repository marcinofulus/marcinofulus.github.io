Trivial SSL in 2D

Let's simulate a basic Self-Supervised task. We have data belonging to 4 hidden classes (represented by Red, Blue, Green, and Yellow dots).

Force 1: SSL Attraction

In SSL (like JEPA), the model learns by bringing "positive pairs" closer together. This force pulls particles of the same color towards their shared center of mass.
Watch the Davies-Bouldin index drop as groups become tight and well-separated.

Force 2: SIGReg Sliced Wasserstein

To prevent collapse, SIGReg projects the points onto 1D lines and compares them to a perfect Gaussian. It pushes the aggregate cloud to expand and fit the Gaussian density.

The Result: The classes remain clustered due to SSL attraction, but SIGReg forces the 4 clusters to spread out and occupy distinct quadrants, solving the representation problem!
