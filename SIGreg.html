🧠 Conceptual Guide

Based on arxiv:2511.08544, this lab illustrates Statistical Invariant Gaussian Regularization (SIGReg).

  • 1. Embeddings: Dots represent high-dimensional vectors in a latent space. Colors represent data classes (e.g., different objects).
  • 2. SSL Attraction: Simulates the Invariance Loss in JEPA. It pulls similar samples together, forming clusters (features).
  • 3. SIGReg: A global force that ensures the total distribution of embeddings matches a target (Gaussian). It prevents all clusters from collapsing into a single point.
"A healthy representation must be discriminative (low DB index) and informative (high variance)."

🧪 How to Experiment

Exp A

Click Trigger Collapse. Observe how SSL pulls same-colored dots together, but without SIGReg, the Global Collapse pulls everything to (0,0). Information is lost.

Exp B

Click SSL + SIGReg. Watch how SIGReg (sky blue slider) fights the collapse. It forces the clusters to spread out and occupy different quadrants while remaining distinct.

Exp C

Switch between Wasserstein and Fourier. Notice how Fourier often "glues" clusters together because it optimizes global density moments rather than specific point positions.

🛠️ Algorithm Internals

▼

1. Sliced Wasserstein (Sorting)

sorted = [...projections].sort();
error = gaussian_quantile[i] - sorted[i];
force = error * projection_vector;

2. Fourier (Epps-Pulley)

re_emp = average(cos(t * projections));
loss = (re_emp - target_gaussian_char_func)^2;
force = -grad(loss);
